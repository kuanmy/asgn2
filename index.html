GLOBAL INTERNET USAGE  as of March, 2021

INTERNET USE RATE by COUNTRY

Internet use rate indicates the number of internet users per 100 people, where internet users are defined as people that has access to the worldwide network (Sandhya, 2021).

From the map on the right, it can be seen that Iceland has the highest internet use rate among all countries, which is around 96. In contrast, East Timor has the lowest internet use rate of 0, indicating that almost no one per 100 people has access to the Internet.

INTERNET USE RATE by CONTINENT

Next, we look into the distribution of the internet use rate for each continent. First, Europe has the highest median internet use rate of 69. This is followed by North America, South America, Asia and Oceania. On the contrary, Africa has the lowest median internet use rate of all, which is around 6.

On the other hand, looking at the min-max range of the internet use rate in each continent, it can be seen that European countries typically have an internet use rate larger than 30. In comparison, Africa countries all have an internet use rate lower than 50. Additionally, among all continents, Asia and Oceania have the largest range of internet use rates, spanning from 0 to 83.

INCOME PER PERSON & URBAN RATE vs INTERNET USE RATE

Lastly, the relationship between income per person and urban rate with internet use rate is explored. Here, income per person indicates the Gross Domestic Product per capita in 2000 US dollars whereas urban rate indicates the percentage of people living in the urban areas (Sandhya, 2021) .

Overall, the larger the income per person of a country, the internet use rate of the country tend to be larger too. For instance, the United States of America has high income per person and internet use rate, Thailand has medium income per person and internet use rate, whereas Iraq has low income per person and internet use rate.
